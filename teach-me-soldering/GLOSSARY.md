# Soldering with Flux Glossary

Core terminology for hand soldering electronics with flux paste and tin solder wire.

## Terms

**Flux**:
A chemical agent that removes metal oxide from surfaces during soldering, allowing molten solder to wet and bond to the base metal.
_Avoid_: paste (ambiguous -- could mean solder paste), cleaner

**Wetting**:
The ability of molten solder to spread across and bond to a metal surface. Good wetting produces a smooth, concave fillet. Poor wetting produces balling or beading.
_Avoid_: sticking, flowing

**No-clean flux**:
A flux type whose residue is non-conductive and non-corrosive, safe to leave on the board. The NC-223-ASM is this type.
_Avoid_: safe flux, clean flux

**Rosin-core solder**:
Solder wire with flux built into a hollow core. The flux releases automatically as the wire melts. Sufficient for most through-hole joints without additional flux.
_Avoid_: regular solder, normal solder

**Fillet**:
The concave, volcano-shaped solder joint formed where a component lead meets a pad. A good fillet is shiny (leaded) or slightly matte (lead-free), smooth, and concave.
_Avoid_: blob, drop, joint shape

**Cold joint**:
A defective solder joint caused by insufficient heat. Looks dull, grainy, or lumpy. Mechanically weak and electrically unreliable.
_Avoid_: bad joint, grey joint

**Bridge**:
An unintended solder connection between two adjacent pads or pins. Common on closely-spaced headers.
_Avoid_: short, blob between pins

**Tinning**:
Applying a thin coat of solder to a surface (tip, wire, pad) before making the final joint. Protects against oxidation and improves heat transfer.
_Avoid_: pre-soldering, coating
