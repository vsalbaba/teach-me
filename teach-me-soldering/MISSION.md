# Mission: Soldering with Flux Paste and Tin Wire

## Why
Build small ESP32 projects confidently. Current soldering attempts come out badly -- specifically, the user doesn't understand how and when to use flux paste. Getting clean, reliable solder joints on through-hole and SMD components for ESP32-based circuits is the goal.

## Success looks like
- Understands what flux does chemically and mechanically, and can explain why it matters
- Can apply NC-223-ASM flux paste correctly before soldering a joint
- Produces clean, shiny, reliable solder joints on ESP32 headers, breakout boards, and common through-hole components
- Knows when a joint is good vs. cold/bridged/dry, and can fix bad joints
- Can solder confidently in an apartment (ventilation, safety, workspace)

## Constraints
- Apartment workspace -- ventilation and fume management matter
- Equipment on hand: soldering iron (unknown model/wattage), NC-223-ASM BGA flux paste, tin solder wire
- Has soldered before but self-rates as bad -- some muscle memory exists, but technique needs correction
- No prior understanding of flux usage

## Out of scope
- BGA rework / reflow (despite having BGA flux)
- PCB design and fabrication
- SMD hot air rework
- Advanced topics like drag soldering fine-pitch QFP
