# Tip care is a habit system, not a repair procedure

The user's recurring problem (tips stop accepting solder, solder beads off) is caused by tip oxidation -- the same oxide chemistry learned in Lesson 1, but applied to the tip itself. The user's old transformer iron likely ran uncontrolled at 450C+, accelerating oxidation beyond recovery.

**Key insight delivered**: Tip care is four habits at four moments (start of session, before each joint, when idle, end of session), all organized around one principle: never leave a hot tip bare. The user also learned the escalation ladder for rescuing oxidized tips (flux -> tip tinner -> brass brush -> replace) and why abrasives destroy tips by stripping iron plating.

**Evidence**: User stated "my old irons always stop attracting solder and it beads and runs off" -- classic oxidation from poor maintenance habits combined with uncontrolled temperature.

**Implications**: The user now has the conceptual framework to maintain tips. Next practical step: confirm they have brass wool (not just a sponge) and optionally tip tinner. May want to do a hands-on session where they practice the full ritual (heat -> clean -> tin -> solder -> tin -> store) on scrap components.
